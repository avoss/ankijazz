Practice Chords

Overview

Many musicians practice scales and scale patterns to get more fluent with playing melodies and solos. This is an equivalent technical exercise for chords: you practice all chord types in all keys systematically. Every exercise focusses either on a certain chord in a certain key, or a common chord progression like II-V-I or Blues. You can use this deck to practice both, comping and soloing.

Cards are sorted by difficulty, where difficulty includes the frequency chords are used in popular music (most common chord types come first), number of different chords in the progression, number of accidentals, tempo of playback and some other criteria. So, you probably don't want to configure Anki to show cards in random order (unless you really want to).

Examples

Description

Fields

The Type field can have the following values:

  • Triads
    Progressions built of Major, Minor, Augmented and Diminished Triads
  • Workouts
    Chord progressions that focus on a single chord. Walks through the most commonly used chords in modern music, including e.g. Minor 7, Major 7, Dominant 7, 7sus4, Altered chords and some substitusions. Although the progressions focus on a single chord type, they are arranged in a way that sound at least somewhat musical.
  • Simple II-V-I Progressions
    II-V-I progressions in Major and Minor with only few chord substitutions
  • Extended II-V-I Progressions
    II-V-I progressions in Major and Minor with more chord substitutions
  • Jazz Blues
    Jazz Blues progressions in Major and Minor
