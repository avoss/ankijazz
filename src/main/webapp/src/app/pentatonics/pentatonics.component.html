Pentatonic Scales

Overview

These decks cover the Minor7 and Minor6 Pentatonic Scales (see below about Minor6 Pentatonic). Minor Pentatonics are very common in many music styles. They can also be used to outline chords and modes (similar to arpeggios). There are five decks with increasing difficulty to learn and practice these:

Description

Minor6 Pentatonic (aka "Robben Ford Scale")

I think it originates from the Blues, e.g. if you play a Blues in A then on the A-Chord you probably play the A-Minor Pentatonic. But when it comes to the D7 chord, the A-Minor Pentatonic does not sound very good because the D7 contains an F# which conflicts with the G. So if you replace the G with F# in the A-Minor Pentatonic you get the A-Minor6 Pentatonic. A great scale to outline 7th and m7b5 chords and also some Melodic Minor stuff because the Melodic Minor scale contains two m6 Pentatonics.
