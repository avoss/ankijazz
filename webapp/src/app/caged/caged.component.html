CAGED Scale Fingerings

Description

Melodic Minor and Harmonic Minor

While CAGED fingerings for the Major scale are pretty much defined, there exist some variations for Melodic and Harmonic Minor. I chose the following:

  • When you play the Dorian mode with a major 7th instead of the minor 7th, you get Melodic Minor. So I took the Dorian fingering and raised the 7th degree by one fret.
  • When you play the Aeolean mode with a major 7th instead of the minor 7th, you get Harmonic Minor. So I took the Aeolean fingering and raised the 7th degree by one fret.

I like this systematic approach because it gives good results and is easy to remember. However, there is one exception to this rule: if the root is on the E-strings, then the fingering becomes easier if you move the major 7th from B-String to E-String too.

Modes and Chords

In both card types, fretboard and jamtrack, the audio plays chords. This is the chord you get by stacking the thirds of the mode with the following exceptions:

  • for Phrygian Dominant (5th mode of Harmonic Minor) I chose a 7b9 chord
  • for Altered Scale (7th mode of Melodic Minor) I chose a 7#5 chord

This also applies to the arpeggios in Level 5.
